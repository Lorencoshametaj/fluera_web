Dweck's work documented how praise for *talent* ("you're so smart") tends to produce a fixed mindset — students avoid challenging tasks because failure would threaten their identity. Praise for *effort and strategy* ("you worked through this carefully") produces a growth mindset, where difficulty is read as information rather than judgement.

The effect is largest precisely where it matters most: when students encounter material at the edge of their ability.

Fluera treats every feedback moment as an opportunity to reinforce growth mindset. Ghost Map frames mismatches as gaps to close, not deficits to mourn. Spaced repetition language celebrates the act of retrieval, not the percentage correct. The AI never tells a student they are "bright" — only that they worked through something hard.
