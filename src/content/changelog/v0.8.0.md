The default FSRS scheduler gets most of the way there. The last 20% — the part that distinguishes a good spaced-repetition system from a great one — is personalisation.

**New**

- Eight FSRS weights (w0–w7) calibrated per-user via gradient descent on review history
- Pedagogical modifiers stacked on top:
  - **Hypercorrection bonus** — confidence-5 wrong answers get shorter next intervals (and a priority badge)
  - **Peek malus** — premature reveals shorten the next interval (no free lookup)
  - **Response-time signal** — slow correct responses treated as weaker than fast correct responses
  - **Breadcrumb scaffolding** — hint chains scale the interval by how much scaffolding was needed
- Per-subject weight calibration (medicine vs math vs languages)

**Improved**

- Review-queue ordering now uses topological sort of prerequisites, not flat date-based
- Post-review feedback celebrates *effort* rather than *correctness* (growth mindset microcopy)

**Thanks**

To everyone in the closed alpha who generated enough review data for the gradient descent to actually converge. The scheduler is better because your reviews were imperfect.
